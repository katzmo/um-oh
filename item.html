Venus of Willendorf

The Venus of Willendorf is a small statuette, estimated to have been carved 29,500 years ago. It was recovered in 1908 from an archeological dig near Willendorf, Austria. The statuette is 11.1 cm (4.4 inches) tall and is made from oolitic limestone, originally colored with red ochre. Similar figures have been found from France to Russia.
